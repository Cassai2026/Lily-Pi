## 2. Head Protection & Impact Safety

Helmets are safety-critical personal protective equipment (PPE).

**Critical warnings:**
- Adding electronics to a helmet can **compromise its structural integrity** and **void its safety certification** (e.g., DOT, ECE 22.06, SNELL, EN 1078)
- **Never cut, drill, or modify the EPS (expanded polystyrene) impact liner**
- **Never attach rigid objects inside the helmet** that could cause secondary injury in a crash
- A Lily Pi–modified helmet **should not be relied upon** to meet the safety standard it was originally certified to
- **Replace any helmet after an impact**, regardless of modifications

If you intend to use this on a public road, consult a certified helmet safety engineer before proceeding.

---

## 3. Electrical Safety

- Lithium polymer (Li-Po) batteries can **catch fire or explode** if overcharged, over-discharged, punctured, or short-circuited
- **Always use a certified battery management system (BMS)** between the Li-Po and the load
- **Never charge a Li-Po pack inside a helmet** or in an enclosed space
- Inspect wiring and connectors before each use
- Do not operate the device in heavy rain unless all electronics are properly sealed (IP67 or better)
- Maximum safe operating voltage for Raspberry Pi GPIO pins is **3.3 V** — exceeding this will permanently damage the SoC

---

## 4. Distraction & Road Safety

A HUD inside a helmet **introduces visual distractions**. Even well-designed HUDs can:
- Reduce situational awareness
- Delay reaction time
- Cause fatigue on long rides

**Recommendations:**
- Never interact with on-screen menus while moving
- Use only glanceable, minimal information (speed, navigation arrow, single-line AI alerts)
- Practice in a safe, stationary environment before road use
- Consider local laws regarding in-helmet displays (see Section 5)
