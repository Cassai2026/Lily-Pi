Stretford Elevated Transport Spine

Concept Proposal for Urban Regeneration & Traffic Management

1. Vision for Stretford

The Stretford Elevated Transport Spine is a proposed 2-lane elevated roadway designed to reduce congestion, improve traffic flow, and revitalise Stretford’s urban spaces. By elevating through-traffic, we aim to:

  • Alleviate matchday congestion at Old Trafford
  • Improve local traffic conditions along Chester Road
  • Free up ground-level space for community use
  • Promote urban regeneration and long-term development

2. Elevated Corridor Design

The 2 × 1 lane traffic decks will run above the existing A56 Chester Road, with precast concrete or steel materials providing a durable, efficient structure. This elevated section will seamlessly connect Stretford M60 with White City and potentially extend toward the Mancunian Way.

Proposed Alignment:

  • Starting near Stretford M60 Interchange
  • Passing along the Stretford Mall and Old Trafford stadium area
  • Continuing toward White City

Traffic Flow:

  • 50 mph speed limit for through-traffic
  • Dedicated stadium access ramp for event days
  • Local traffic reallocated to ground level for safer pedestrian access and cycling lanes

3. Undercroft Activation: Community and Green Space

The space beneath the elevated transport spine will be activated for public use, including:

  • Pop-up markets
  • Youth recreation zones (skate parks, basketball courts)
  • Community workshops
  • Event spaces for matchdays and local festivals
  • Cycle and pedestrian pathways safe from traffic

4. Stadium & Regeneration Integration

This elevated transport spine would directly support Manchester United’s redevelopment plans, by providing:

  • Reduced matchday congestion and smoother event access
  • New fan zone spaces under the bridge
  • Better pedestrian safety around the stadium and local areas

5. Sustainable Build Process

By using local materials and a modular construction approach, we can complete the project efficiently while reducing environmental impact.

  • Recycled aggregates and concrete sourced locally
  • Modular components for faster construction and minimal disruption
  • Night-time construction to reduce daytime traffic disruptions

6. Phased Implementation

This project will unfold in three key phases:

  • Phase 1: Pilot section (2000m) from Stretford M60 to White City
  • Phase 2: Traffic modelling, assessment, and expansion towards the Mancunian Way
  • Phase 3: Full-scale implementation and regeneration along the A56 corridor
